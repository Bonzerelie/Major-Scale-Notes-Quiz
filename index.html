    More information 🧐

    The Major scale forms the foundation of the vast majority of Western music, across many genres. In short, a musical scale is the path of notes we take from one pitch to another, generally (but not always) spanninng an octave, after which they will usually repeat.
    Specifically the Major scale can be built using a formula of tones and semitons (called whole-steps and half-steps for our North American friends) from any one pitch to the same pitch an octave above.

    This major scale formula can be written as:
    T T S T T T S
    (or for our friends in the US and Canada)
    W W H W W W H

    In this quiz you select the 7 different notes that belong to the Major scale in question. You can choose to select all 8 notes in a full octave (ie the root and the 8th scale degree an octave above), this will also be counted as correct!
